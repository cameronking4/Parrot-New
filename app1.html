Mission

We're building the first true social network without comparative metrics. We’re a platform that helps facilitate relationships that would otherwise not have developed due to fear, anxiety, or plain awkwardness. You don't "meet on Parrot" like a dating app, rather interest is simply validated.

In the status quo, other apps pigeonhole users into substitutes that do not cater towards the full scope and nuances of modern relationships. Users currently have dating apps and their corresponding premium subscriptions to validate interest online, and have even resorted to featureless web apps whose sole values are answering anonymous questions in order to bring to light their curiosities.

We learned that students are put off by dating apps, and rather they often rely on mutual connections with their crushes to determine whether or not there exists the potential for a match.

In our one-click world, there’s no one-click solution for validating interest without Parrot. Current social networks serve as de facto dating apps for our generation. However, none offer a concrete, one-click method of validating interest that still allows users to save face.

Dating apps all help people discover new matches outside of their circle. People don't always want to meet someone entirely random online. In addition, matching with someone you know on these platforms may feel awkward and frequently hinders their users from engaging in real life. There’s no follow-through.

The act of swiping mindlessly, the pressure to create “the perfect profile”, and a lack of engagement outside of messaging is ineffective and outdated. Those apprehensive of the idea of dating apps are left solely with direct messages via Instagram, Snapchat, or other text-based communications. This is a high-risk and stressful way to vet whether or not someone is actually interested. So this is why there's a need for Parrot.

Why Parrot?

"Swiper No Swiping!" 🦜

Dating apps aim to connect users with random locals in your area. Parrot is completely different. Instead of being reduced to swipe cards, we empower our user to take ownership of their match request by chirping people they know and see in real life.

We're building a platform that:

  • Empowers introverts with anonymity, choice, and enhanced safety precautions
  • Present users in a way that does not reduce them to swipe cards
  • Is crafted and designed for college students
  • Mimics how people already use social platforms
  • Solves the pain point of sending the first message
  • Takes the stress out of over digital curation
